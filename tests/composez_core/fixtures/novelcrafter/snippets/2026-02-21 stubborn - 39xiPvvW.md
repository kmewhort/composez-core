The hum of the dehumidifier was the only pulse the room possessed—a mechanical heart beating for a library of corpses. Elias sat beneath the cone of his task lamp, the light a surgical white against the grey, skin-like leaves of the *Vernazza Codex*.

He did not breathe as he took up the bone folder. To disturb the air was to risk the integrity of the dust. With a precision born of a decade’s penance, he teased the tip of the tool beneath a flap of peeling goatskin. The leather resisted with a dry, papery shriek that vibrated up his arm and settled in his teeth.

"You are being stubborn tonight," he murmured, his voice a flat, uncontracted rasp in the silence.

He reached for a jar of wheat starch paste, the scent of fermented grain cutting through the heavy musk of vanilla and old iron. As he dipped his brush, he noticed a tremor in his left hand—not a flinch of muscle, but a rhythmic twitch that felt like a pulse beneath a page. He looked down. The UV light on the corner of the desk flickered, and for a fraction of a second, the anatomical diagrams hidden beneath the shipping manifests seemed to inhale.

The grey paper didn't just feel like skin; it was beginning to radiate a faint, impossible warmth.

He pulled his hand back, the bone folder clattering onto the slate table. The sound was too loud, a gunshot in a cathedral. From the darkness of the flat files, beyond the reach of his lamp, came the unmistakable friction of silk sliding against stone.

"The binding is original," a voice drifted from the shadows, smooth and devoid of heat. "It would be a shame to mar it with such a clumsy repair, Mr. Thorne."
