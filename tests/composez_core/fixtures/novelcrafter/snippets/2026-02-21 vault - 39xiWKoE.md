The air in the vault had turned to liquid ink. Elias staggered between the stacks, his shoulder catching on the iron edge of a shelf, but he felt no pain—only the sensation of a smudge on a page. His left arm was translucent now; he could see the titles of the books through his own forearm, the Latin scripts vibrating like trapped insects behind his radius and ulna.

He reached the center of the room where the **Debt Collector** lay open. It was no longer blank.

The pages were gorging themselves. Black veins branched across the white paper, siphoning the light from the room, pulling the very color from Elias’s skin. He looked down at his hands and saw not flesh, but a series of hurried charcoal sketches that were beginning to flake away into the intake of the floor vents.

"Stop," he wheezed. The word felt heavy, a leaden type-set falling from his mouth. "You are... erasing the wrong witness."

Across the table, the figure from the shadows—no longer a stranger, but a mirror—smiled. The figure’s face was a perfect restoration of Elias’s own, but without the ink-stains, without the silver-rimmed glasses, without the burden of a single remembered sorrow.

"The debt must be balanced, Elias," his double said, the voice echoing as if spoken into a deep well. "You spent your life stabilizing the past. Now, the past is stabilizing you. You aren't the restorer anymore."

Elias looked at the *Vernazza Codex* one last time. The three puncture wounds in its spine were bleeding—not blood, but a thick, iridescent bile that smelled of woodsmoke and a childhood summer he could no longer quite name. He realized then that the "anatomical diagrams" he had seen under the UV light hadn't been for monsters.

They were blueprints for him. He was the final page, and the ink was finally dry.
